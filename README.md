# AGI Timeline — Job Displacement Model

**Live site:** [jacobjake1.github.io/agi-timeline](https://jacobjake1.github.io/agi-timeline/)

An interactive model of US job displacement after ASI public deployment. Built on the premise that AGI and ASI are functionally one event — by the time a public announcement is made, ASI already exists internally. The gap is hours, not months. Recursive self-improvement becomes more and more exponential the moment the loop closes.

---

## What this models

- **Wave 1 — Cognitive displacement** (Day 1 through Month 9): knowledge work, white collar, professional services, logistics, transportation, engineering, government, retail — displaced by ASI capability alone
- **Wave 2 — Humanoid robot & autonomous vehicle wave** (Month 6–18): physical labor and driving roles displaced as ASI directs simultaneous robotic and AV deployment
- **Split productivity multiplier**: Wave 1 ÷5.0 — one person with AGI does the work of 5+ knowledge workers, so companies never hire replacements. Headcount collapses through non-replacement not direct cuts. Wave 2 ÷1.5 — robot/AV wave is physically constrained.
- **Cascade Lost**: every direct job eliminated removes ~0.55 additional downstream jobs through spending collapse in restaurants, retail, and local services. Total Effective = Direct + Cascade — the real number of Americans who lose income.

---

## Core assumptions

- AGI arrival: **2027–2029** (Dario Amodei's internal estimate from inside Anthropic: 2026–2027. Most AI engineers working on the systems themselves say 2027–2028. 2028 is the modal estimate weighting all signals.)
- AGI and ASI are one event separated by hours via recursive self-improvement
- ASI is deployed at full capability day one — no ramp up
- Immediate displacement window is **30 days**, not 90 — contract non-renewals happen fast when the capability gap is unambiguous
- US workforce baseline: **160M**
- Cascade multiplier: **×0.55** indirect job losses from spending collapse

---

## The headcount compression argument

AGI doesn't just replace individual workers — it collapses entire departmental headcounts. 1 person + AGI replaces 50. A 500-person company becomes 10 people overnight. Not from malice — from fiduciary duty. The CFO presents the math to the board, the decision happens in one meeting. The competitive cascade forces every sector simultaneously: the moment one company in a sector announces 60% cost reduction, every competitor faces the same existential choice within one or two quarterly cycles. No CEO can afford to wait.

---

## What this doesn't model

Jobs that survive and increase in value: live officiating, coaching, physical presence roles, creative arts, genuine human connection work. These compress in supply and rise in price as the automated world creates demand for authentically human experiences.
