# AGI Timeline — Job Displacement Model

**Live site:** [jacobjake1.github.io/agi-timeline](https://jacobjake1.github.io/agi-timeline/)

An interactive model of US job displacement after ASI public deployment. Built on the premise that AGI and ASI are functionally one event and that by the time a public announcement is made, ASI already exists internally. The gap is hours, not months. Recursive self improvement quicker becomes more and more expoentional.

---

## What this models

- **Wave 1 — Software displacement** (Day 1 through Month 9): knowledge work, white collar, professional services, logistics, government, retail — displaced by ASI capability alone
- **Wave 2 — Humanoid robot wave** (Month 6–18): physical labor displaced as ASI directs simultaneous robotic deployment
- **Productivity multiplier ×2.0**: one worker with ASI replaces 5+ people not because jobs disappear directly, but because ASI makes each surviving worker so productive that companies never hire replacements. Headcount collapses through non-replacement, not just direct cuts.

---

## Core assumptions

- AGI arrival: **2027–2029** (Dario Amodei's internal estimate: 2026–2027) (Multi Interal AI Engineers, the ones working on the systems themselves all mostly say 27-28)
- AGI and ASI are one event separated by hours via recursive self-improvement
- ASI is deployed at full capability day one — no ramp up
- Immediate displacement window is **30 days**, not 90 — contract non-renewals happen fast when the capability gap is unambiguous
- US workforce baseline: **160M**

---

## What this doesn't model

Jobs that survive and increase in value: live officiating, coaching, physical presence roles, creative arts, genuine human connection work. These compress in supply and rise in price as the automated world creates demand for authentically human experiences.
