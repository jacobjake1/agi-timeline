The replacement decision doesn't require AGI. It requires: AI cost < human cost AND quality ≥ 70%. Both conditions are already met for a large share of routine knowledge work. The displacement already happening with today's imperfect AI is the evidence for that — not a projection. AGI would remove most of the remaining exceptions on top of this, which is what Section 3 below reasons about.

Anthropic's own published data (Jun 2026): the share of job tasks AI could theoretically perform is large across nearly every category. Actual measured usage is far smaller — but the gap is what's closing, not whether it exists.

Blue = theoretical task coverage (AI could do this). Green = observed actual usage today — this is real, measured data, not a projection. The size of the blue bars is real and confirmed.
